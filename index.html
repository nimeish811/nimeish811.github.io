What is FRP/GRP?

FRP stands for Fiber Reinforced Polymer and GRP stands for Glass Reinforced Polymer. It is a reinforced plastic material containing glass fibers in a resin matrix.

Fiberglass is light, durable, strong, impact resistant, corrosion resistant and has a moderate high strength-to-weight ratio. It can be made into various shapes and offers excellent thermal insulation properties.

The growing trend of replacing heavy metal components with lighter fiberglass composites is creating immense growth opportunities — and AKT Enterprise is at the forefront of this revolution.

FRP/GRP Structural Profile Types

All cross-section profiles can be pultruded & customized using Polyester, ISO or Vinyl ester resin systems with different forms of fiberglass reinforcements.

C Channel

Structural channel section used for framing, supports and brackets in industrial applications.

Box Pipe

Hollow square/rectangular section offering excellent torsional rigidity for structural frameworks.

I-Beam

Classic I-section beam for load-bearing structures, walkways and mezzanine platforms.

L-Angle

Equal and unequal angle sections used for corner joints, supports and edge protection.

Round Pipe

Circular hollow section ideal for handrail systems, conduit runs and structural columns.

Polyester Rod

Solid round rod used for rebar, insulating pins, spacers and specialty structural applications.

Flat Section

Flat bar sections used for grating supports, stiffeners, kick plates and cladding applications.

Deck Panel

Structural deck panels for flooring, roofing and walkway decking in corrosive environments.

Kick Plate

Protective plates installed at the base of walkways, platforms and stairways for foot-level safety.

Why Choose FRP/GRP?

FRP composites outperform conventional metals across every critical performance parameter.

🛡️ Corrosion Resistant

Withstands all types of chemical attacks, making it ideal for aggressive industrial environments.

⚖️ Lightweight

Up to 4× lighter than steel, reducing handling, transport and installation costs significantly.

💪 High Strength

Tensile strength up to 1200 MPa — far exceeding steel's 390 MPa performance.

🔌 Non-Conductive

Electrically and thermally non-conductive — safe for electrical utilities and insulation applications.

🔧 Maintenance Free

No painting, coating or cleaning required — dramatically reducing lifecycle costs.

♻️ Long Life

Predicted durability of 80–100+ years, delivering the lowest lifecycle cost of any structural material.

☀️ UV Resistant

Built-in UV resistance protects against direct sunlight exposure without degradation.

🎨 Customizable

Available in custom sizes, shapes and colors as per RAL — tailored to your exact project needs.
